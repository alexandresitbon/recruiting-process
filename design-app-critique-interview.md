# Interview objective

The app critique interview is designed to understand a candidate's strengths in how they think about and evaluate designs. These critiques are an essential part of the interview process because they allow candidates to showcase their knowledge while allowing us to gauge their ability to think critically about design problems and provide constructive feedback on them.

# Interview prompt

>I'd like for us to walk through Google Maps and to get your thoughts on the visual design, interaction design, what could be improved, etc. Let's reverse-engineer the app and try to figure out why they made the decisions they made.

# Evaluating Candidates

A good process for a candidate includes:
1. **Setting Context:** They talk about the company, what problem the app solves, or other contextual topics. It shows that you can think holistically.
2. **Walkin Through a Flow**: They Identify a use case and walk through that flow, analyzing the design and how well it solves the problem
3. **Evaluating The Flow**: They summarize their thoughts on the flow and how well it solves the problem you identified
4. **Evaluating the Business**: They articulate the following: How does this app or company make money? What's the business model? What metrics do they think the app creators care about?

# Interview rubric

Score each area 1–4. Use the descriptors to anchor your score. Fill out immediately after the interview.

## 1. Product & Business Thinking

Can they zoom out beyond the UI to think about the company, market, business model, and metrics?

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| ----- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | No mention of business context, company mission, or metrics. Critique stays entirely at the surface level of the UI.                                                                            |
| 2     | Acknowledges the company or product category but stays vague. Business model or metrics mentioned in passing without meaningful connection to design decisions.                                 |
| 3     | Clearly articulates what problem the company solves, how it makes money, and connects at least some design decisions back to likely business goals or metrics.                                  |
| 4     | Demonstrates sharp product thinking throughout. Proactively identifies business model, key metrics, and competitive positioning — and consistently uses this lens to evaluate design decisions. |

## 2. User Empathy & Use Case Identification

Do they go beyond their own experience to identify multiple distinct user types and jobs-to-be-done?

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                               |
| ----- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Only speaks from their own experience. No acknowledgment of other user types or use cases.                                                                               |
| 2     | Identifies one or two use cases but stays close to the obvious. Limited perspective-taking beyond themselves.                                                            |
| 3     | Proactively identifies multiple distinct user types and use cases, including ones they don't personally relate to.                                                       |
| 4     | Demonstrates genuine empathy across a range of user types. Frames design observations in terms of how well the app serves different users, not just personal preference. |

## 3. Flow Selection & Problem Framing

Do they pick a meaningful use case to anchor the critique, and consistently tie design observations back to how well it solves that problem?

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| ----- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | No clear flow selected. Critique jumps around with no connective thread.                                                                                                                            |
| 2     | Selects a flow but drifts away from it. Design observations feel disconnected from a central problem or user need.                                                                                  |
| 3     | Selects a clear, meaningful flow and mostly ties observations back to how well the app serves the identified use case.                                                                              |
| 4     | Flow selection feels deliberate and insightful. Every design observation is anchored to the user problem — nothing feels arbitrary. The structure makes the critique easy to follow and persuasive. |

## 4. Visual Design Craft

Can they speak credibly and specifically about typography, color, spacing, information hierarchy, and the design system as a whole?

|Score|Descriptor|
|---|---|
|1|Visual design observations are generic ("it looks clean," "I like the colors"). No use of design vocabulary or specific examples.|
|2|Some specific observations but inconsistent. May note one or two visual elements without connecting them to the broader design system or user experience.|
|3|Speaks with confidence about type, color, spacing, and hierarchy using specific examples. Evaluates individual elements in the context of the overall design system.|
|4|Demonstrates exceptional visual craft. Goes deep on specific design choices, explains how they work together as a system, and offers precise, well-reasoned opinions on what's working and what isn't.|

## 5. Interaction Design Depth

Do they notice and articulate micro-interactions, motion design, and animation — the details that separate strong craft from surface-level observation?

|Score|Descriptor|
|---|---|
|1|No mention of interaction design, micro-interactions, or motion. Critique stays at the static screen level.|
|2|Acknowledges interactions exist but stays high-level ("the animations feel smooth"). No specific observations about timing, easing, or behavior.|
|3|Calls out specific micro-interactions or motion patterns and explains why they work or don't. Demonstrates awareness that interaction design is a distinct craft.|
|4|Goes deep on interaction design with precision — discusses easing, timing, state transitions, feedback loops, and how motion contributes to (or detracts from) the overall experience.|

## 6. Designer Empathy & Intentionality

When they critique something, do they offer rationale for why it might be designed that way? Do they demonstrate awareness of real-world tradeoffs?

|Score|Descriptor|
|---|---|
|1|Critique is one-sided — only positive or only negative. No attempt to understand why design decisions were made.|
|2|Occasionally acknowledges tradeoffs but in a generic way ("I'm sure they had reasons"). Doesn't draw on real experience or demonstrate genuine understanding of design constraints.|
|3|Regularly follows critiques with plausible rationale. References real constraints like technical limitations, metrics pressure, or research findings to explain design decisions.|
|4|Consistently demonstrates a both-sides perspective. Draws on personal experience shipping compromises to make the rationale feel credible and specific. Shows genuine respect for the original designer's decisions even when disagreeing.|

## 7. Communication & Structure

Do they stay organized, move through the critique with purpose, and avoid jumping to conclusions too quickly?

|Score|Descriptor|
|---|---|
|1|Disorganized and hard to follow. Jumps straight into critiquing UI without setting context. Thoughts feel scattered.|
|2|Some structure but uneven pacing. Either rushes through context or gets stuck and loses the thread.|
|3|Moves through context → flow → evaluation in a logical sequence. Easy to follow. Manages time reasonably well.|
|4|Exceptionally clear and well-paced. Sets context confidently, builds a coherent narrative through the critique, and wraps up with a crisp summary. Feels like someone who has thought deeply about communication as part of their craft.|

## Hiring Signal

| Total Score | Signal     |
| ----------- | ---------- |
| 24–28       | Strong Yes |
| 18–23       | Yes        |
| 12–17       | No         |
| Below 12    | Strong No  |
