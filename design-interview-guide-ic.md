## Overview

We're hiring a senior to staff level product designer to work embedded in small, autonomous teams alongside engineers and product managers — owning projects from discovery through shipping. The interview process is structured to surface not just design craft and taste, but the character, judgment, and cross-functional operating style that determine whether someone will thrive in a small, fast-moving team where designers are expected to lead from within.

## Interview loop

| Round | Format  | Who            | Duration | Description            | What It Assesses                                                      |
| ----- | ------- | -------------- | -------- | ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Virtual | Recruiter      | 30 min   | Recruiter screen       | Background, motivations, role fit                                     |
| 2     | Virtual | Hiring manager | 60 min   | Hiring manager screen  | Character and values (behavioral) + design thinking (app critique)    |
| 3a    | On-site | Design team    | 60 min   | Portfolio presentation | Portfolio depth, craft, process, product thinking                     |
| 3b    | On-site | Design team    | 45 min   | Whiteboard challenge   | Problem framing, design process, decisiveness under ambiguity         |
| 3c    | On-site | PM + Engineer  | 45 min   | Behavioral interview   | Cross-functional operating style, collaboration, influence, ownership |
| 4     | Virtual | Daniel (CEO)   | 30 min   | Final interview        | Mission alignment, leadership potential, culture fit                  |

## Rounds Description

This is meant for the recruiter or hiring manager to send to candidates to make it clear to them what they can expect in each round of the process. 

### Round 2

This is a great opportunity to connect with your hiring manager and get a real feel for the role! The 60-minute interview covers two parts: first, a conversation about your background, the work you're most proud of, and how you've navigated challenges in past roles; then a live design critique exercise where you'll walk through an app together. There's no right or wrong answer in the second part — we're genuinely curious about how you think, what you notice, and how you articulate your point of view on design decisions.

### Round 3

This is an exciting on-site interview where you'll get to meet and work with members of both the design and product teams! The day covers three sessions: you'll kick things off by walking through projects from your portfolio, then roll up your sleeves for a hands-on design challenge where you'll work through a problem collaboratively, and wrap up with a conversation with the product team about how you work cross-functionally. Come ready to talk about your work in depth and to think on your feet — it's a great opportunity to get a real feel for how we work and who we are as a team.

Specifically for the portfolio presentation, you will have a 60 minute time slot to present your portfolio to people who will interview you throughout the day. Along with 2 or 3 case studies that show your work and professional development, make sure to share a little about your background and how you think about and approach design. 

Along with a demonstration of your hard skills (visual design, interaction design, copywriting, etc.), we seek to understand your mindset and how you approach tackling design challenges, including: 

- Incorporating empathy and user understanding 
- Awareness of broader business context for the work
- How you overcome inevitable challenges and obstacles
- How you generate ideas and design directions 
- Your relationship to other team members 
- How you persuade others of the soundness of your decisions

Plan on about 45 minutes of presentation, and then 15 minutes for discussion afterward.

### Round 4

This is a final 30-minute conversation with Daniel, Coast's CEO, and a chance for both of you to connect directly before a decision is made. Come ready to talk about what draws you to Coast's mission and where you want to take your career — it's as much an opportunity for you to ask questions as it is for Daniel to get to know you.

## Interviewer expectations

**Before the interview:**
- Make yourself available to the recruiter and hiring manager. Unless you are dealing with a high severity issue, Interviewing candidate is a top priority takes precedence over everything else
- Review the candidate's resume, LinkedIn profile and portfolio
- Review the interview objective, questions, and rubric

**During the interview:** 
- Start on time and introduce yourself briefly.
- Ask your assigned questions and let the candidate answer fully before following up. 
- Push for specificity when answers are vague: ask for a real example, a specific decision, or a concrete outcome. 
- Take notes as you go so your rubric scores are grounded in what was actually said, not a general impression.
- Always Be Selling (ABC): when you introduce yourself or when you answer questions demonstrate your excitement for the company, the product, our team, and you role. You want to make candidates feel that our team is right for them. 

**After the interview** 
- Complete your Greenhouse write-up - including your rubric scores - within the same day of the interview and before the debrief.
- Come to the debrief with a clear recommendation and supportive evidence. 
- Do not discuss your scores or impressions with other interviewers before the debrief to avoid anchoring bias.
