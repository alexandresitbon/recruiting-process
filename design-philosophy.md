# Coast Product Design Philosophy

## What design Is

Design at Coast is a foundational, collaborative discipline - alongside product management and engineering - that shapes quality and intent. In other words, the role of design at Coast is not to "make it pop" or move pixels along Figma files. 

We treat design as central to building opinionated, high-quality products and services for a specific audience: fleet drivers and operators. We do not build "everything for everyone." We make strong choices for a clear use case, and we make them well.

## What we believe

### Great design is opinionated
We start with a strong point of view on the right workflow for our users. This creates better products and differentiated experiences. We resist the temptation to design by committee. A clear perspective, executed with conviction, beats a flexible solution that pleases no one.

### Great design is craft
We see craft as the human act of shaping something with care and putting part of yourself into the work. In a world where most products prioritize speed over polish, craft is a competitive advantage. Every detail (e.g., a transition, an empty state, an error message) is an opportunity to show our users that we care.

### Great design accelerates growth and creates delight
We believe design drives business results first before being aesthetics. Design it not decoration. It's a lever for growth, including: 
- Attracting attention: customers notice and trust beautiful, professional products
- Improving clarity and onboarding: reducing friction from first contact
- Increasing engagement and retention: giving customers reasons to stay

## How we work

### Quality is the north star
Every design decision is evaluated by whether it improves overall quality and the customer experience. We favor deliberate, polished work over "move fast and break things." We ship when it's right, not just when it's done.

### No handoffs — whole-team iteration
We operate in small cross-functional teams (typically 3–5 people) collaborate from concept through completion, iterating together toward what feels right. Design does not throw work over a wall to engineering. Everyone is accountable for the final experience.

### Design leads from discovery
Design is not brought in after the problem is defined. Designers are involved from the earliest stages of discovery; framing the problem, challenging assumptions, and helping define what success looks like before a single pixel is placed.

## What this means for designers at coast

We hire designers who:
- Have a strong point of view and the conviction to defend it
- Care deeply about craft and sweat the details others overlook
- Think in systems, not screens
- Understand that their work has a job to do; attract, convert, retain
- Thrive in small, collaborative teams and hold themselves accountable for outcomes, not just outputs
