# Design portfolio review at application stage

Time budget: 5 minutes max. You're pattern-matching for fit, not doing a deep eval.

## Step 1 — Resume scan

Before touching the portfolio, orient yourself.

- [ ] What companies/products have they worked on?
- [ ] What level do they appear to be? Does it match the role?
- [ ] Any obvious red flags (huge gaps, no real companies, only freelance/agency)?

## Step 2 — Portfolio landing page

First impression of the portfolio itself as a designed artifact.

- [ ] Does it load and work without friction?
- [ ] Does the about/positioning statement tell you who they are and what they care about or is it generic?
- [ ] Does the portfolio feel like it was built for a specific audience, or is it a dumping ground of everything they've ever done?
- [ ] Is the work visible immediately, or buried?

## Step 3 — Case study selection

Pick one case study to dig into — either the first one listed, or the one most relevant to the role.

- [ ] Is there any work that's immediately relevant? 
- [ ] Are the projects real shipped work — or mostly speculative redesigns and concept exercises? 
- [ ] How many projects are shown? A focused 2–3 is a green flag. 10+ with no curation is a yellow flag.

## Step 4 — Case study scan

Scan section headers, bolded text, and images. Don't read everything.

### Context

- [ ] Is their specific role clearly stated? (Lead, contributor, one of five?)
- [ ] Is the timeframe clear?
- [ ] Who did they collaborate with? (PM, Eng, research?)

### Process

- [ ] Does the case study show how they moved from problem to solution — or does it just show the final output?
- [ ] Is there evidence of real design thinking: sketches, iterations, decisions, tradeoffs? 
- [ ] Do the artifacts shown (research, wireframes, prototypes) feel meaningful to the process, or like they were added to check a box?

### Craft & output

- [ ] Are the final designs well-organized and visually solid? (Typography, spacing, color, hierarchy)
- [ ] Any typos, pixelated images, or broken UI?

### Outcomes

- [ ] Is there any mention of results or impact? Doesn't need to be a metric — even qualitative outcomes count.
- [ ] Do they acknowledge what didn't work or what they learned? 

## Step 5 — Level calibration

Adjust what you're looking for based on the role level.

- [ ] **Mid-level**: Can they execute on a significant feature independently and explain their impact?
- [ ] **Senior/Principal**: Can they navigate ambiguity, make consequential decisions, and show influence beyond their immediate work?

## Step 6 — About section

Quick gut check on the person behind the work.

- [ ] Does their personality come through? _(Tanner: hiring managers want to know why you do the work you do)_
- [ ] Does their stated direction/interest align with what we're building at Coast?

## Quick disqualifiers

Stop if any of these are true:
- No portfolio linked
- Password-protected with no password provided
- Portfolio is 100% speculative/conceptual work with no real shipped projects
- No evidence of process — just final screens
- Work is clearly not in the right domain or complexity tier for the role

## Overall signal check

After 5 minutes, ask yourself:

- Does this person have the right shape for this role?
- Is there at least one project that makes me want to ask them questions?
- Would I be able to tell their work apart from the other 10 portfolios I'm reviewing today?

If yes to all three → move forward. If uncertain on one → note it as a question for the recruiter screen, don't auto-reject.
