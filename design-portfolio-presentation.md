# Interview objective

The goal of the portfolio presentation is to get a sense of a candidate's product thinking and visual design and interaction design craft. But it is also meant to know if the candidate is a proactive and driven member of a team. And it is mean to get a sense for who the candidate is as a person.

# Suggested prompts/questions

*Candidates will drive this with their presentation. Feel free to ask clarifying questions, but try not to interrupt the portfolio presentation with too many questions, as there will be time after the presentation for Q&A and discussion.*

To make sure you’re getting the most out of the time you have with the candidate, keep the following questions in mind, and use them to maintain focus. Not all of these questions will be relevant for all roles. The Hiring Manager should make clear what skills are pertinent and worth probing on. 

- What was your role? 
	- Who else was on the team and what was your relationship to them?
- What were the goals of the project? 
	- How did you deliver on them? 
	- How did you deem the success of your work? (Don't lead the candidate towards any specific types of answers, but look to see if they talk about the work in a business context, including specific measures of success, or if they talk about it more generally)
- How was user research used to inform the designs? 
	- How were you involved in conducting that research?
- What design standards and guidelines did you work within? 
- What role did content strategy and copywriting play, and how involved were you with that? 
- To what level of fidelity did you deliver these designs? 
	- Were static comps made, or dynamic prototypes? 
	- How were these designs built? (Again, we're not wanting to lead here, but we are curious about the candidate's technical savvy, including whether they deliver production-ready front-end development.)
- How was the design process and approach for the work established? 
	- Were they any obstacles or hiccups in the process? How did you overcome them?
- Who were the stakeholders for this work? 
	- What was your role in engaging with them?

# Interview rubric

Score each area 1–5. Anchors are provided at 1, 3, and 5 — use 2 and 4 for scores between anchors. Use N/A for craft skills that are not relevant to the role. If a skill is relevant but the candidate didn't demonstrate it, score a 1.

## Craft Skills

### Visual / UI Design

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| ----- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: Unappealing, stale, untrained. No understanding of hierarchy within design elements.                                                                                                                                                          |
| 3     | Average: Doesn't create new visual systems, but can work well within existing systems, guidelines, and with oversight.                                                                                                                              |
| 5     | Superior: Creates compelling new visual systems and visual languages. Brings simplicity and depth to design through deep understanding of hierarchy among design elements. Strong style guide development; work that is inventive, inspiring, bold. |

### Interaction Design

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| ----- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Poor: Interactions not thought through, obvious flaws, preferences appearance over use.                                                                                                    |
| 3     | Average: Workmanlike workflows and wireframes, gets the job done.                                                                                                                          |
| 5     | Superior: Tames complexity admirably, can wrangle from the details of microinteractions to the complexity of digital products and multi-channel ecosystems, including marketing and email. |

### Copywriting / Content Strategy

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                           |
| ----- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: Unclear use of language; poor grammar, uses 'lorem ipsum'.                                                                                                     |
| 3     | Average: Competent use of copy, language, and terminology throughout the interface.                                                                                  |
| 5     | Superior: Uses content as a design material; creates experiences that leverage content; can define tone and voice standards and patterns across digital experiences. |

### Information Architecture

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                            |
| ----- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: Unclear, muddled navigation; poor search/filtering schemes.                                                                                     |
| 3     | Average: Proficiency with large content systems, successful application of IA best practices around labeling, navigation, and filters.                |
| 5     | Superior: Created schemes for handling large information systems; inventive application of information architecture in digital and physical contexts. |

### User Research

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| ----- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: Relies on outdated or simplistic user research tools; can't articulate why you'd use one tool instead of another.                                                                              |
| 3     | Average: Uses data gathering tools (interviews, diary studies) and analysis and insights tools (personas, journey maps) with competency.                                                             |
| 5     | Superior: Able to take a research objective and craft a specific program to get at exactly what the research is hoping for; proven ability for research to inform and drive product decision making. |

### Prototyping

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| ----- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: Doesn't use prototyping in their digital design work.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| 3     | Average: Proficiency with basic prototyping tools and practices; nothing flashy, but makes the effort to move beyond static representations to something dynamic and usable.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| 5     | Superior: Excels at making prototypes that feel like the real thing, and can do so quickly; comfortable tapping into real data sources to drive prototypes; able to develop a prototyping infrastructure that enables efficient ongoing prototype development; able to participate in prototyping to facilitate design thinking and evaluation, making prototyping part of the design process, not just for user testing. |

### Front-end Development

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| ----- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: Little to no working knowledge of HTML, CSS, and the technology underlying the design.                                                                                                                                                                          |
| 3     | Average: Basic knowledge of front-end technologies; comfortable coding their own designs.                                                                                                                                                                             |
| 5     | Superior: Treats code as the design medium; comfortable with advanced practices with libraries, jQuery, Node/React/etc; can commit production-ready front-end code; pays close attention to detail in implementing designs, concerned with fit and finish and polish. |

## Mindset / Interpersonal Skills

_There is no N/A for these characteristics — all are applicable. If the candidate's work doesn't demonstrate a characteristic, that warrants a low score._

### Empathetic / User-Focused

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                    |
| ----- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: No demonstration of user understanding or orientation.                                                                                  |
| 3     | Average: Draws on basic knowledge of user needs and capabilities to inform design work.                                                       |
| 5     | Superior: Empathy and user understanding drives impressive new thinking and new solutions, and is validated through success once it launches. |

### Detail-Oriented

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| ----- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: No deep or detailed thinking; superficial; clear they haven't thought through matters when probed; lack of consistency across solutions.                                                                                                       |
| 3     | Average: Decent level of rigor; understands how their design choices impact larger project.                                                                                                                                                          |
| 5     | Superior: Delights in the importance of details; can spec their own work to the highest degree; system thinker who not only thinks through every permutation of their design, but also considers how their design fits within the broader ecosystem. |

### Business Savvy

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| ----- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: No demonstration of how business context informed design.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| 3     | Average: Basic level understanding of key metrics like conversion, and how well designs performed to deliver business value.                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| 5     | Superior: Strong strategic bent; can use design work to help make/drive a business case; comfortable working with partners — Product, Tech, Marketing/Brand, Operations — to understand priorities and drive collaborative engagement to deliver high quality customer experience and meet the goals of the business. |

### Acknowledges / Navigates Tradeoffs

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                          |
| ----- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: Considers only one thing when making design choices; doesn't acknowledge multiple factors (business, tech, and user needs) that need to be balanced.          |
| 3     | Average: Able to make basic tradeoffs between different factors when looking for the best solution to a problem; recognizes there's no 'best' way.                  |
| 5     | Superior: By keeping eyes focused on desired outcomes and results, nimbly able to make tradeoffs between competing factors, always maintaining a focus on the goal. |

### Drives Solutions and Projects

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                        |
| ----- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: Doesn't initiate anything; receives requirements and plans from others.                                                                     |
| 3     | Average: Plans their own work; potentially leads a team.                                                                                          |
| 5     | Superior: Classic bias to action — sees a problem, crafts a plan, gets people on board, and tackles it; can effectively lead people and programs. |

### Creativity / Generativity

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                       |
| ----- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: Doesn't come up with multiple solutions to a problem; solutions are predictable.                                                                           |
| 3     | Average: Comes up with multiple approaches for solving a problem; still tends to play it safe, though might have the occasional insightful new solution.         |
| 5     | Superior: Extremely creative/inventive; tireless in generating ideas; arrives at solutions that are unpredictable, but still solve the problem; true innovators. |

### Presentation / Communication

| Score | Descriptor                                                                                                                                                     |
| ----- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Poor: Poor, awkward communicator; has trouble articulating thoughts; no narrative to their presentation.                                                       |
| 3     | Average: Solid communicator; thoughtful; might not be persuasive, but can help people follow the logic and rationale behind decisions.                         |
| 5     | Superior: Compelling and charismatic; unexpected uses of storytelling that contribute to their presentation; thoughtful and able to address questions handily. |
