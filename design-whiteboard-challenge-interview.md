# Interview objective

The whiteboard design challenge is designed to observe a candidate's design process, communication, and collaboration skills live — in a format that mimics a real working session. The goal isn't to arrive at a polished solution, but to assess how a candidate scopes ambiguous problems, makes decisions under pressure, and works with others to move toward a solution.

# Interview prompt

>Podcasts are increasingly long — some run 3 hours or more. Design an experience that helps someone get value from a long podcast without listening to the whole thing.


# Evaluating Candidates

- A solid process for a candidate is to set the context > create a persona > design the experience > summarize > 
- We're looking for them to showcase their design thinking, including: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, test, implement

# Interview rubric

Score each area 1–4. Use the descriptors to anchor your score. Fill out immediately after the interview.

## 1. Problem Scoping & Context Setting

Do they ask smart questions upfront to narrow the prompt into something workable, without rushing to a solution?

|Score|Descriptor|
|---|---|
|1|Jumps straight into designing with little or no context-setting. Questions, if any, are surface-level or off-target.|
|2|Asks a few questions but misses key ones (e.g. platform, success metrics, constraints). Moves on before the problem is sufficiently scoped.|
|3|Asks a solid set of questions covering goals, metrics, platform, and constraints. Writes down the prompt and checks for alignment before moving forward.|
|4|Sharp, efficient context-setting. Questions are specific, well-prioritized, and show that they care more about understanding the problem than rushing to a solution. Leaves this stage with a clearly scoped problem.|

## 2. User Definition

Do they define a believable, relevant persona — and do their questions go beyond demographics to behaviors and use cases specific to the prompt?

|Score|Descriptor|
|---|---|
|1|Skips user definition entirely, or defines a persona based purely on stereotypes and basic demographics with no connection to the prompt.|
|2|Defines a persona but stays at the demographic level (age, income, location) without asking about behaviors, habits, or use cases relevant to the problem.|
|3|Builds a persona that goes beyond demographics — asks about behaviors, frequency of use, and context-specific factors that will meaningfully inform design decisions.|
|4|Defines a crisp, believable persona with questions that are clearly tailored to the prompt. The persona feels like a genuine design tool, not a checkbox — and it's easy to trace later design decisions back to it.|

## 3. Design Process & Decisiveness

Do they move through the problem logically and make clear decisions when faced with ambiguity, rather than stalling or rambling?

|Score|Descriptor|
|---|---|
|1|Process is disorganized or non-existent. Stalls frequently in ambiguity and struggles to make decisions without being prompted.|
|2|Shows some process but loses focus or gets stuck. Makes decisions but without clear rationale, or waits for the interviewer to tell them what to do next.|
|3|Moves through the problem with a clear, logical sequence. Makes decisions confidently, articulates the rationale, and keeps momentum even when the path isn't obvious.|
|4|Demonstrates an exceptionally clean design process. Decisive without being rigid — adapts when new information arises but never loses the thread. Makes the process feel natural and effortless.|

## 4. Solution Translation

Do they connect the constraints and goals defined upfront to the user flow and key screens they design?

|Score|Descriptor|
|---|---|
|1|The solution feels disconnected from the context and persona defined earlier. User flow or screens are missing, or don't reflect the problem being solved.|
|2|Some connection between context and solution, but it's loose. Either the user flow or the key screens are underdeveloped, or the design doesn't clearly map to the goals and constraints identified.|
|3|The user flow and key screens feel grounded in the context and persona set earlier. Design decisions are clearly tied back to the goals and constraints identified upfront.|
|4|The solution is a direct, coherent expression of everything defined in the earlier steps. Every design decision feels intentional and traceable. The key screens bring the user flow to life in a way that clearly reflects both user needs and business goals.|

## 5. Collaboration & Communication

Do they treat the interviewer as a partner — checking in, thinking out loud, and inviting input — rather than going heads-down and presenting at the end?

|Score|Descriptor|
|---|---|
|1|Works in silence for long stretches. Treats the interviewer as an audience rather than a collaborator. Little to no thinking out loud.|
|2|Checks in occasionally but inconsistently. Thinking out loud happens in bursts rather than as a natural habit. Collaboration feels performative rather than genuine.|
|3|Thinks out loud consistently and checks in with the interviewer at natural moments. Invites input and uses feedback to move forward. The session feels like a working session, not a presentation.|
|4|Exceptional collaborator. Makes the interviewer feel like a genuine partner throughout — asks for their perspective, incorporates their input, and creates a dynamic that mirrors what a real working session would feel like.|

## 6. Reflection & Summary

Do they leave time to recap, acknowledge tradeoffs and mistakes, and articulate what they'd do next?

|Score|Descriptor|
|---|---|
|1|No recap. Runs out of time or ends abruptly without summarizing. No reflection on what went well or what they'd do differently.|
|2|Attempts a summary but it's thin — mostly restates what was designed without reflecting on tradeoffs, mistakes, or next steps.|
|3|Leaves time for a clear recap that covers what was accomplished, acknowledges at least one thing they'd do differently, and articulates a next step (e.g. testing, iteration, edge cases to explore).|
|4|Recap is crisp and genuinely insightful. Acknowledges mistakes without being self-deprecating, identifies a compelling alternative direction they could have taken, and articulates specific, thoughtful next steps. Leaves the interviewer with a strong impression of their self-awareness and growth mindset.|

## Hiring signal

| Total Score | Signal     |
| ----------- | ---------- |
| 20–24       | Strong Yes |
| 15–19       | Yes        |
| 9–14        | No         |
| Below 9     | Strong No  |
